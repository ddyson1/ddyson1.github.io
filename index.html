Current work

Moodlog is an AI-powered journaling platform built on the premise that the language people use about their own lives, read carefully, reveals emotional structure they may not see themselves. Users write freely; the platform reads each entry and tags the feelings it finds, surfacing emotions, people, and patterns over time.

It pairs a fine-tuned RoBERTa emotion model with named-entity recognition to map entries across Plutchik's eight primary emotions, on a production stack of Django, PostgreSQL, Docker, and AWS. Built end to end and self-funded, without venture capital. Invite-only as of mid-2026.

Other projects

  • MiG – a native iOS app that trains vocabulary for learners of Russian through speed-reading drills; 1,500+ curated words across CEFR A1–B1.
  • Plotflow – an independent pen-plotter studio producing Universal Century mobile suits drawn in ink by machine, in signed limited editions.
  • worldView – a morning dashboard distilling global markets and geopolitics into a single daily view.

About

Devin Dyson is a computer scientist and web developer in Boston. He holds a Master of Science in Computer Science from Boston University, with interests in affective computing and natural language processing, and previously ran operations for the Digital Currency Initiative at the MIT Media Lab.

He builds products end to end — research, model, infrastructure, and business model — and draws with pen plotters.
